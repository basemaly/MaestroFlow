# Persuade Critique

This skill applies a practical argumentative rubric inspired by persuasive-writing annotation frameworks.

## Rubric

- Position or thesis: Is the central stance clear?
- Claims: Do the supporting claims actually advance the thesis?
- Evidence: Is support concrete, relevant, and sufficient?
- Counterclaims: Does the draft acknowledge real opposition?
- Rebuttals: Does it answer opposing points convincingly?
- Conclusion: Does the ending reinforce the reasoning rather than merely repeat it?

## Workflow

1. Extract the thesis or main position.
2. Map each major claim and the evidence attached to it.
3. Identify counterclaims and rebuttals, if any.
4. Flag unsupported assertions, weak evidence, and logical gaps.
5. Recommend the highest-leverage revisions first.

## Output

- Overall persuasive strength
- Argument map
- Weak points
- Suggested revisions
