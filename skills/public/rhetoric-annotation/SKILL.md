# Rhetoric Annotation

Use this skill to explain how rhetorical choices affect persuasion.

## What To Look For

- Framing devices that sharpen or weaken the thesis
- Contrast, parallelism, repetition, amplification, and emphasis
- Places where the prose gestures at persuasion without actually supporting the claim
- Missing rhetorical moves that would make the argument more forceful

## Workflow

1. Identify the text's persuasive goal.
2. Mark the strongest rhetorical devices actually present.
3. Explain how each device helps or hurts persuasion.
4. Point out weak passages where rhetoric substitutes for substance.
5. Recommend specific rewrites that improve both rhetoric and clarity.

## Output Shape

- Short overall assessment
- Rhetorical devices observed
- Weak or missing rhetorical moves
- Revision advice
